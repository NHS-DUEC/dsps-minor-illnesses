Genitals and bottom includes problems peeing or pooing and sexual health problems

Choose the one symptom that's bothering you most today.

Genitals

  1. Catheter problems

    A problem with a blocked or leaking catheter. Pain from a catheter. Trouble removing or inserting a catheter.

  2. Genital injury

    An injury anywhere on the genitals in the last 7 days that hasn't cut the skin.

  3. Genital injury with cut or wound

    An injury to the genitals within the last 7 days that has cut the skin. Cuts, wounds or grazes anywhere on the vagina or the area between the anus and vagina.

  4. Bleeding from the vagina

    Heavy periods, bleeding after sex or unexpected bleeding. Bleeding or spotting when pregnant. Any bleeding after the menopause.

  5. Discharge from the vagina

    Vaginal discharge, with or without tummy pain.

  6. Itchy vagina

    Sore or itchy vagina.

  7. Male sexual problems

    A physical or emotional problem with sexual health or activity. Worries about sexually transmitted infections (STIs), HIV/AIDS

  8. Penis, testicle or scrotum problems

    Male genital problems, with or without tummy pain.

  9. Problems with peeing

    Pain, stinging or burning while peeing. peeing too often or smelly pee.

  10. Sexual or period problems
    Checks for things like: emergency contraception, contraception, STIs, menopause

    Menopause. Hot flushes. Sexual identity. PMS. PMT. Sexual assault. Menopausal or premenstrual problems. Sexually transmitted infections. HIV/AIDs.

  11. Vaginal lump or swelling

    Any lump, cyst, swelling or pain in or around the vagina. Swollen or sore labia.

  12. Problems peeing

    Pain, stinging or burning when peeing. Only being able to pee very small amounts, or cannot pee at all. Bloody, red or pink pee. Peeing too much or smelly pee.

  13. Something stuck in or around the penis

    An object stuck inside or around the penis.

  14. Something stuck in the vagina

    An item such as a tampon, cap, condom or other object, that's got stuck in the vagina.

Bottom

  1. Bleeding from the bottom

    Spots of blood on the toilet paper. Streaks of blood in poo. Red or black coloured poo.

  2. Bottom pain, swelling lump or itch

    Pain, itching or swelling in or around the bum (anus or rectum). Lumps in or around the bottom.

  3. Constipation

    Can't poo or finding it hard to poo, with or without tummy pain.

  4. Diarrhoea

    Runny or watery poo, with or without pain in the tummy.

  5. Loss of bowel control

    An urge to poo that can't be controlled. Soiling or staining underwear. May also include constipation or diarrhoea, bloating or wind.

  6. Something stuck in the bottom

    An object stuck in the back passage.

  7. Stoma problems

    A problem with any stoma or bag attached to the body.
