All topics

  1. Accidentally taking something harmful Accidentally taking, swallowing, breathing in or injecting something poisonous or harmful. Taking too much of any medicine by mistake.
  2. Ankle or foot injury An injury to the ankle or foot that doesn't have any type of cut or wound. Sprained ankle.
  3. Ankle or foot injury with a cut or wound An injury to the ankle or foot that has any type of cut or wound.
  4. Any problem affecting the skin Includes bites and stings, moles, rashes and allergies
  5. Arm or shoulder injury An injury to the arm or shoulder within the last 7 days, that has not cut the skin. A dislocated shoulder or arm.
  6. Arm or shoulder injury with a cut or wound An injury to the arm or shoulder in the last 7 days, that has caused a cut or wound.
  7. Behaving strangely Any odd behaviour. Seeing or hearing things that aren’t there. Can’t stay awake. New changes or an existing condition getting worse.
  8. Bites or stings A bite or sting from animals, humans, insects, birds or reptiles. Swollen, itchy, red, reaction. Bitten by a dog, cat, a pet or wild animal. Stung by a wasp, bee, mosquito or horsefly. Spider, tick or bites from bugs. Burning or stinging pain. Irritation. Stinging plants and nettles. Lump, swelling or blister.
  9. Bleeding from the bottom Spots of blood on the toilet paper. Streaks of blood in poo. Red or black coloured poo.
  10. Bleeding from the vagina Heavy periods, bleeding after sex or unexpected bleeding. Bleeding or spotting when pregnant. Any bleeding after the menopause.
  11. Blisters Blisters from something rubbing against the skin, such as new shoes.
  12. Blocked or stuffy nose A congested or blocked nose. Sinus problems.
  13. Blood in pee (urine) Bloody, red or pink pee (urine)
  14. Bottom pain, swelling lump or itch Pain, itching or swelling in or around the bum (anus or rectum). Lumps in or around the bottom.
  15. Breast and nipple problems Any problems with the breast or nipple. Lumps, pain or any worries about skin changes.
  16. Breastfeeding problems Problems getting the baby to attach to the breast. Cracked or sore nipples, or a painful breast that is making breastfeeding difficult.
  17. Breathing problems Checks for things like: asthma, chest infection, pneumonia, bronchitis, angina Wheezing, panting or feeling out of breath. Difficult or noisy breathing.
  18. Burns from heat A scald from hot liquid or a burn from touching something hot, such as an iron or a flame.
  19. Can't feel baby move as much Worries that the baby isn't moving as much as usual. Baby's movements are slower or have stopped.
  20. Catheter problems A problem with a blocked or leaking catheter. Pain from a catheter. Trouble removing or inserting a catheter.
  21. Chemical burns Burns caused by chemicals.
  22. Chest or upper back injury An injury to the chest or upper back within the last 7 days, that hasn't cut the skin. Broken, painful or bruised ribs. Injuries from the collarbone to bottom of the ribcage.
  23. Chest or upper back injury with a cut or wound A wound on the chest or upper back (the collarbone to the bottom of the ribcage) within the last 7 days. Broken, painful or bruised ribs with a wound.
  24. Chest pain Checks for things like: heartburn, angina Any chest or upper back pain. Chest pain with or without arm, shoulder, neck or jaw pain. Tightness in the chest.
  25. Cold arm or arm changing colour A cold, painless arm. Dead arm. White, blue, or black colour in one or both arms.
  26. Cold leg or leg changing colour A cold, painless leg. Dead leg. White, blue or black colour in one or both legs.
  27. Cold or flu A blocked or runny nose. Sneezing, aching muscles or headache. Fever (high temperature), coughing, sore throat or pain in the face.
  28. Constipation Can't poo or finding it hard to poo, with or without tummy pain.
  29. Cough Checks for things like: chest infection, hay fever Hacking, tickly or dry cough, without other cold symptoms. Coughing up phlegm or mucus.
  30. Coughing up blood Coughing up blood in sick (vomit), phlegm or mucus.
  31. Cuts, scratches or grazes A small cut, scratch or graze that might have gravel, dirt or grit inside.
  32. Dental injuries Dental problems caused by an injury, such as knocked out or broken teeth. Injuries to the gums or the inside of the mouth.
  33. Diabetic blood sugar problems Diabetic blood sugar levels are too high or too low. A sudden problem with blood sugar levels.
  34. Diarrhoea Runny or watery poo, with or without pain in the tummy.
  35. Diarrhoea and vomiting Runny or watery poo and being sick, with or without tummy pain.
  36. Difficulty swallowing Having trouble swallowing or unable to swallow, without a sore throat
  37. Difficulty peeing Problems with peeing (passing urine), such as only being able to pee very small amounts, or can't pee at all.
  38. Discharge from the vagina Vaginal discharge, with or without tummy pain.
  39. Dizziness Feeling dizzy, off balance, lightheaded or as if the room is spinning (vertigo).
  40. Drink and drug problems Alcohol or substance misuse. Seeing or hearing things that aren't there. Trouble sleeping.
  41. Drunk too much alcohol Too much to drink. Being sick or feeling ill because of alcoholic drinks.
  42. Earache Ear pain, swelling, or fluid leaking from the ear. A painful blocked ear. New deafness with pain. Ringing in the ear.
  43. Eye injury An injury to the eye or eye socket within the last 7 days without any cut or wound.
  44. Eye injury with a cut An injury to the eye or eye socket within the last 7 days that has caused a cut or wound.
  45. Eye or eyelid problems Sore, yellow, red or bloodshot eyes. Loss or changes in vision. Floaters or flashes. Blurred vision. Swollen, sticky, itchy or sore eyelids. A lump on the eyelid.
  46. Falling, fainting or passing out A fall, faint, blackout or loss of consciousness, with no injury.
  47. Fever or high temperature Chills and shivering, or hot to touch. A high temperature, with no other symptoms.
  48. Finger or thumb injury A finger or thumb injury within the last 7 days that hasn't cut the skin.
  49. Finger or thumb injury with a cut or wound A finger or thumb injury within the last 7 days that has cut the skin.
  50. Fingernail injury Any injury to the fingernail or nails in the last 7 days.
  51. Fluid or wax coming from the ear with no loss of hearing Fluid, ear wax or watery discharge from one or both ears but without any loss of hearing.
  52. Genital injury An injury anywhere on the genitals in the last 7 days that hasn't cut the skin.
  53. Genital injury with cut or wound An injury to the genitals within the last 7 days that has cut the skin. Cuts, wounds or grazes anywhere on the vagina or the area between the anus and vagina.
  54. Glue on the skin Something is glued to the skin, or the skin has been glued together.
  55. Groin pain or swelling Pain or swelling in the groin (between the waist and upper thighs).
  56. Hair falling out or thinning Hair loss from any part of the body. Losing hair in patches or clumps.
  57. Hand or wrist injury A hand or wrist injury in the last 7 days that hasn't cut the skin.
  58. Hand or wrist injury with a cut or wound An injury to the hand or wrist within the last 7 days that has cut the skin.
  59. Head, face or neck injury An injury to the head, face or neck within the last 7 days that didn't cut the skin.
  60. Head, face or neck injury with a cut or wound An injury to any part of the face (but not eyes or teeth), head or neck, which has cut the skin, within the last 7 days.
  61. Headache or migraine Throbbing or intense pain or a tight band around the head. Visual problems like flashing lights with a headache.
  62. Hearing problems or blocked ear Hearing problems or deafness without earache. Blocked ear or fluid leaking from the ear with no pain.
  63. Heatstroke Overheating caused by being in hot temperatures or by exercising too much.
  64. Hiccups Hiccups, with possible fever. Hiccups won't stop.
  65. Itchy vagina Sore or itchy vagina.
  66. Late or missed period Late or missed periods. No periods. Periods haven't started. Periods have stopped.
  67. Leg injury A leg injury to the hip, thigh, knee, calf, shin or ankle within the last 7 days that didn't cut the skin.
  68. Leg injury with a cut or wound An injury to the leg, hip, thigh, knee, calf, shin or ankle in the last 7 days, that has a cut, wound or graze.
  69. Locked or aching jaw Problems opening or closing the mouth or jaw.
  70. Loss of bowel control An urge to poo that can't be controlled. Soiling or staining underwear. May also include constipation or diarrhoea, bloating or wind.
  71. Loss or change to your sense of smell or taste You cannot smell or taste anything, or things smell or taste different to normal.
  72. Lower back injury An injury to the lower back (below the rib cage) or bottom in the last 7 days that hasn't cut the skin.
  73. Lower back injury with a cut or wound An injury to the lower back (below the rib cage) or bottom in the last 7 days with a cut, wound or graze.
  74. Lower back pain Pain or ache in the lower part of the back.
  75. Male sexual problems A physical or emotional problem with sexual health or activity. Worries about sexually transmitted infections (STIs), HIV/AIDS
  76. Mental health Low mood, feeling depressed or stressed. Panic attacks or anxiety. Trouble sleeping. Seeing or hearing things that aren’t there. Drink and drug problems.
  77. Mouth ulcers Mouth, lip, tongue or gum ulcers. Sore white or grey patches inside the mouth.
  78. Nosebleed A bleeding nose.
  79. Nosebleed after an injury A bleeding nose following an injury.
  80. Older wound problems Any problem with a wound that was treated by a doctor or nurse more than 7 days ago
  81. Pain in the side of the body Pain on either side of the body, between the ribs and hips. Affecting one side only at the back (love handles) or with stomach pain.
  82. Palpitations Feeling the heart is beating too fast or too slow. Pounding or irregular heartbeat. Racing heart, fluttering in the chest, skipped beats.
  83. Penis, testicle or scrotum problems Male genital problems, with or without tummy pain.
  84. Problems with peeing (passing urine) Pain, stinging or burning while peeing (passing urine). peeing too often or smelly pee.
  85. Problems with drains, stitches, plaster casts or metal rods New pain or problems with stitches, staples, plaster casts, metal rods, drainage tubes or central line catheters that were inserted less than 7 days ago.
  86. Self-harm or overdose Self harm (cutting, burning, substance abuse, anorexia, bulimia or suicide attempt) as a way of dealing with difficult feelings. Taking too many pills on purpose. Swallowing, breathing in or injecting something poisonous to harm yourself.
  87. Sexual or period problems Checks for things like: emergency contraception, contraception, STIs, menopause Menopause. Hot flushes. Sexual identity. PMS. PMT. Sexual assault. Menopausal or premenstrual problems. Sexually transmitted infections. HIV/AIDs.
  88. Shoulder problems Ache or stiffness in one or both shoulders. Unusual feelings or weakness.
  89. Sinusitis A problem with the sinuses. Face pain around the cheeks, eyes or forehead. A runny nose and a high temperature.
  90. Sleep problems Problems sleeping, getting to sleep or staying asleep. Tiredness. Insomnia.
  91. Something in the ear An object stuck inside the ear. An insect in the ear.
  92. Something in the eye Dirt, dust or an object has gone into the eyes. Something sprayed or splashed into the eyes.
  93. Something stuck in or around the penis An object stuck inside or around the penis.
  94. Something stuck in the nose An object stuck inside a nostril.
  95. Something stuck in the vagina An item such as a tampon, cap, condom or other object, that's got stuck in the vagina.
  96. Sore throat A painful throat, with or without problems swallowing. A hoarse, croaky or lost voice, with or without a sore throat.
  97. Splinters or problems with piercings A problem with a small object stuck in the skin. Wood or glass splinters. Problems with piercings.
  98. Stoma problems A problem with any stoma or bag attached to the body.
  99. Stomach ache or pain Checks for things like: indigestion, heartburn, food poisoning, stomach ulcer, gallstones, kidney stones Tummy pain with or without symptoms such as being sick, diarrhoea or a high temperature. Trapped wind. Abdominal pain. Swollen stomach.
  100. Stomach or side injury An injury to the tummy, stomach or sides within the last 7 days that hasn't cut the skin. Stomach injury, flank injury.
  101. Stomach or side injury with a cut or wound A wound to the tummy, stomach or side within the last 7 days. Flank injury. Stomach injury.
  102. Sunburn Burns caused by the sun or a tanning sunbed or lamp. Red, warm, sore, tender, or peeling skin.
  103. Swallowed or breathed in an object Accidentally swallowing or inhaling something. Choking or inhaling an object. Something stuck in the throat.
  104. Swollen or painful arm Pain or swelling in the arm or elbow.
  105. Swollen or painful face or neck Swelling, lumps or pain in the face or neck. Swollen glands.
  106. Swollen or painful hand, finger or wrist Pain or swelling in the hand, fingers, nails or wrist.
  107. Swollen or painful legs, knees, ankles or feet Checks for things like: sciatica, gout, ingrown toenail, cramp, ulcers Painful or swollen legs, thighs, knees, ankles or feet. Pain or swelling in the hips or bum. Heel pain.
  108. Tiredness Feeling tired or exhausted (fatigue) most of the time, or tiring easily.
  109. Toe injury A toe injury within the last 7 days that hasn't cut the skin.
  110. Toe injury with a cut or wound An injury to one or more toes within the last 7 days that has cut the skin.
  111. Toenail injury An injury to one or more toenails within the last 7 days. Painful, broken, ingrown or loose toenails.
  113. Toothache, swelling and other dental problems Bleeding from the mouth, teeth or gums. Painful teeth or gums. Swelling or lumps on the gums, mouth or jaw. A crown, bridge, fillings, denture or brace problem. A broken tooth, or teeth not caused by an injury.
  115. Toothache after an injury Toothache and pain caused by an injury, such as broken, cracked or knocked out teeth. Bleeding or injuries to the gums or mouth. Swelling or lumps on the face or jaw. Problems opening or closing the mouth.
  116. Trembling or shaking Shaking or trembling (tremor).
  117. Unusual skin or body sensations Strange feelings in the skin anywhere on the body. Numbness, burning, tingling or pins and needles.
  118. Vaginal lump or swelling Any lump, cyst, swelling or pain in or around the vagina. Swollen or sore labia.
  119. Vomiting or nausea Being or feeling sick.
